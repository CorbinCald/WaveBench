Where do the foreground layers sit and how big are they?

Since the extra layers are "in front of and lower than" the main wave, each layer's baseline drops further down the frame. The open question is amplitude: do the foreground layers crest as tall as the main wave, or are they smaller/flatter to feel like closer water near shore?

A

Damped foreground (amp shrinks per layer) ← recommended

Main = full amplitude. Layer 1 ≈ 0.80×. Layer 2 ≈ 0.65×. Foreground layers feel like closer, flatter water — subtle swells tracking the main wave without poking up into it. Clean depth, main wave remains the visual star.

B

Equal amplitude, stacked

All three layers same amp, just positioned lower. Foreground layers' crests poke up aggressively — may feel like "competing" peaks. Strongest sense of depth but noisiest silhouette.

C

Amplified foreground (closer = bigger)

Foreground layers have larger amplitudes, like waves closer to the viewer looking bigger. Main wave appears as a smaller swell behind taller foreground surf. Dramatic, but inverts your "main wave is the star" framing.

Baseline offset (shared by all options): Each layer's center-line drops by roughly 15% of frame height. So Layer 1's calm point sits ~15% lower than main; Layer 2's sits ~30% lower. Their crests still peak above their own baseline, but each layer occupies a different vertical band.
